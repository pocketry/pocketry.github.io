Shae's Letter to Autumn

Autumn,

My heart aches for you and I mourn all the small perfect moments we will never have together.

My finger feels the lack of your little hand like a physical weight. I yearn to feel your sweet head on my chest and the weight of you in my arms. And the smell of your head when I lean down to kiss it. My body produces milk, but it will not nourish you or help you to grow. I will give it elsewhere to make it useful. To help me forgive myself.
I catch glimpses of life with you in it...in my arms as I sing your brother to sleep, wrapped up carried close to my chest while we share a meal, in the swing that still sits in your big sister’s room - empty as we play on the floor with magnets or blocks, you staring up with love and fascination while your brother plays or sings or shares a story book with you. These things break me. Because we can never have them.
You took a piece of my soul with you when you left us. I hope it was enough to guide and comfort you. I would have given so much more if it meant that you could stay. Everything. We saturated those brief minutes with all the love we could. I got to hold you, and kiss you, and smell you. And share the small comforts of song and touch. That will never be enough. And it will be enough.

I will ache for you every day for the rest of my life, baby girl. I’ll love you forever, I’ll like you for always. As long as I’m living my baby you’ll be.

Ashlyn's Poem to Autumn

To Autumn Florence Foster
March 25, 2020
I'll love you always.
You were wanted so badly, little girl. My sweet angel niece.
Come to me any time and my heart will hold you.

Invitation to Love by Paul Laurence Dunbar

Come when the nights are bright with stars
Or come when the moon is mellow;
Come when the sun his golden bars
Drops on the hay-field yellow.
Come in the twilight soft and gray,
Come in the night or come in the day,
Come, O love, whene’er you may,
And you are welcome, welcome.

You are sweet, O Love, dear Love,
You are soft as the nesting dove.
Come to my heart and bring it to rest
As the bird flies home to its welcome nest.

Come when my heart is full of grief
Or when my heart is merry;
Come with the falling of the leaf
Or with the redd’ning cherry.
Come when the year’s first blossom blows,
Come when the summer gleams and glows,
Come with the winter’s drifting snows,
And you are welcome, welcome.
